Choosing a hair transplant training program is a significant investment in your medical career. Understanding what you actually get for your money is critical. This page breaks down HTTC pricing, what is included, and how it compares to other major training programs worldwide.

HTTC Training Cost

HTTC training costs USD 5,000 per day. Every training day includes:

  • Real hands-on surgical practice · You perform FUE extractions and implantations on actual patients
  • Direct surgeon supervision · One-on-one guidance from instructors with 20+ years of FUE experience
  • Full theoretical instruction · Hair anatomy, donor management, hairline design, patient assessment
  • Instrument training · Manual punches, the PCID from Cole Instruments, implantation tools
  • All surgical tools and consumables · Nothing extra to pay for equipment or materials
  • Certificate of completion · Signed by HTTC instructors
  • Accommodation assistance · Help arranging nearby hotels in Ankara

Training sessions are available as 1-day intensive courses up to 4-week comprehensive programs, in one-on-one or small group format (2-4 participants).

Why Hands-On Training Matters

Hair transplant surgery is a manual skill. You cannot learn it by watching · just as you cannot learn to operate by watching surgical videos. The difference between observation-based training and hands-on training is the difference between knowing what FUE extraction looks like and knowing how it feels: the resistance of tissue, the angle of the punch, the tactile feedback that tells you the graft is free.

At USD 5,000 per day, HTTC costs more per session than an ASAHRS observation workshop at USD 2,850. But consider what each dollar buys:

  • ASAHRS (USD 2,850): You sit in an audience. You watch a surgeon on a screen. You may ask questions during designated Q&A. You go home having watched, not practiced.
  • HTTC (USD 5,000): You scrub in. You extract grafts. You make recipient channels. You implant grafts. You get real-time correction from a surgeon standing next to you. You go home with actual surgical reps under your belt.

For physicians serious about building a hair transplant practice, the question is not whether USD 5,000/day is expensive · it is whether observation-only training at any price prepares you to operate on your first patient.

Why Turkey for FUE Training

Turkey is one of the few countries where hands-on training on real patients is legally permitted. This is the primary reason why HTTC can offer what most other programs cannot: actual surgical practice during training.

Additional advantages of training at HTTC in Ankara:

  • High patient volume · HLC Hairline Clinic operates daily, ensuring training participants always have cases to work on
  • Direct international flights · Ankara Esenboga Airport (ESB) has connections from Europe, the UK, the Middle East and beyond
  • Cost-effective travel · Ankara offers significantly lower accommodation and living costs compared to Western European or US training locations
  • Central location · Easy access from the UK, Germany, the Gulf states and other countries where demand for qualified hair transplant surgeons is growing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is USD 5,000/day the total cost, or are there additional fees?

USD 5,000/day is the complete training fee. All instruments, consumables, theoretical materials and certificate are included. Participants only need to cover their own travel and accommodation.

How many days should I book?

For beginners with no prior FUE experience: 3-5 days minimum to cover extraction, implantation and independent practice. For experienced surgeons looking to refine technique or learn the PCID: 1-2 days may be sufficient. Contact us to discuss your specific needs.

Can I try a 1-day session before committing to more?

Yes. Many participants book a 1-day intensive to evaluate the training format, then extend on-site if they want additional days.

Do you offer group discounts?

Small group workshops (2-4 participants) can be arranged at adjusted rates. Contact us for details.

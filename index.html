What the training covers

From patient planning to graft placement and follow-up.

A serious FUE course should not reduce training to punching grafts. Doctors need to understand donor management, graft handling, recipient-site planning, implantation angles and post-operative responsibility.

01

Patient selection

Assessing candidacy, expectations and contraindications before any plan is made.

02

Donor assessment

Evaluating donor density, safe zones and long-term sustainability of harvest.

03

Hairline planning

Designing natural hairlines proportioned to the face, age and progression.

04

FUE extraction

Punch selection, angle control and graft handling to protect follicle viability.

05

Implantation strategy

Recipient-site creation, angle and density decisions for a believable result.

06

Aftercare workflow

Post-operative responsibility, follow-up protocols and patient communication.

✓

Every stage is taught hands-on inside the HLC Hairline Clinic, not by slides.

Before you apply

Questions doctors ask before booking.

If something is not covered here, send your profile and we will answer directly before you confirm a place.

Ask a question →
Who is the training for?

HTTC programmes are built for doctors and physicians who want structured, hands-on FUE exposure inside a working clinical environment, not observation-only conference attendees.

Where does the training take place?

All training is delivered in Ankara, Turkey at the HLC Hairline Clinic environment, alongside experienced FUE hair restoration specialists.

How hands-on is the course?

The course covers the full clinical workflow, from patient selection and donor assessment through extraction, implantation strategy and aftercare, with direct faculty supervision rather than slides.

What language is the training delivered in?

Training and clinical discussion are conducted in English. Share your preferred format when you apply and we will confirm what fits your level.

What do I need to send before booking?

Send your current surgical experience and specialty, your target level (observation, hands-on or full workflow), preferred dates and any specific FUE techniques you want to focus on.

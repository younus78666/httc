FUE Training Course

Hair transplant training course for doctors and surgeons

Unlike most programs that limit participants to observation, HTTC participants perform actual extractions and implantations on real patients from day one, under the direct supervision of surgeons with over 20 years of FUE experience.

The curriculum

What you will learn.

The program covers the complete hair transplant workflow, from initial patient assessment to post-operative care.

A

Theory

  • Hair and scalp anatomy, growth cycles, donor physiology
  • Patient selection and consultation techniques
  • Donor area management and long-term planning
  • Hairline design: natural angles, density, age-appropriate
  • Graft survival factors and storage solutions
B

Hands-on surgery

  • Manual FUE extraction, punch 0.6-0.9 mm, depth and angle control
  • Powered extraction with the PCID, transection below 2.58%
  • Stick-and-place implantation for maximum graft survival
  • Body hair transplant from beard, chest and other sites
  • Slit creation: blade, depth and angle for natural density
C

Instruments

  • Manual punch handles, sharp vs. dull techniques
  • The PCID device: setup, calibration, practice
  • Cole Instruments line: punches, processing board, counter
  • Implantation tools, forceps and implanters
Inside the day

A typical training day.

Structured from morning theory through live surgery to an end-of-day debrief. Every day is hands-on.

  • Morning (08:00 – 09:00)

    Theoretical session: anatomy review, technique discussion, case planning for the day's patient(s).

  • Morning surgery (09:00 – 13:00)

    First surgical session. Participants perform FUE extractions under direct supervision, with real-time feedback on punch angle, depth and speed.

  • Lunch break (13:00 – 14:00)

    Review of morning performance and discussion of cases.

  • Afternoon surgery (14:00 – 18:00)

    Implantation session. Participants create recipient channels and perform stick-and-place implantation, focused on density, angle and natural distribution.

  • Debrief (18:00 – 18:30)

    Review of the day, technique assessment, preparation for the next day.

Why HTTC

Real practice, not just observation.

The critical difference between HTTC and virtually every other hair transplant training program is simple: you operate. Participants perform actual surgical steps, extraction and implantation, on real patients. This is not a simulation. This is not a cadaver lab. This is live surgery under expert supervision.

Most courses from organizations like ASAHRS or DHI Academy charge comparable or higher fees for observation-only programs. At HTTC you hold the instruments. You extract grafts. You implant them. You learn by doing.

Turkey is one of the few countries where hands-on training on real patients is legally permitted, which is why leading surgeons from the USA, Europe, the Middle East, Latin America and Asia choose HTTC.

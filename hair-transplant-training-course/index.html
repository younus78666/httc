The curriculum

What You Will Learn

The training program covers the complete hair transplant workflow, from initial patient assessment to post-operative care.

Theory

  • Hair and scalp anatomy, hair growth cycles, donor area physiology
  • Patient selection criteria and consultation techniques
  • Donor area management and long-term planning
  • Hairline design principles – natural angles, density distribution, age-appropriate design
  • Graft survival factors and storage solutions

Hands-On Surgical Training

  • Manual FUE extraction – Punch selection (0.6mm to 0.9mm), depth control, angle adaptation for different hair types (straight, curly, wavy)
  • Powered extraction – Training with the PCID (Powered Cole Isolation Device) from Cole Instruments, achieving transection rates below 2.58%
  • Stick-and-place implantation – Immediate implantation after channel creation for maximum graft survival
  • Body hair transplant (BHT) – Extraction from beard (sub-chin area), chest and other donor sites
  • Slit creation – Blade selection, channel depth and angle for optimal density and natural growth direction

Instrument Training

  • Manual punch handles and sharp vs. dull punch techniques
  • The PCID device – setup, calibration, hands-on practice
  • Cole Instruments product line – surrounded punches, graft processing board, counting device
  • Implantation tools – forceps technique, implanters, stick-and-place methodology

Why HTTC

Why HTTC – Real Practice, Not Just Observation

The critical difference between HTTC and virtually every other hair transplant training program in the world is simple: you operate. At HTTC, participants perform actual surgical steps – extraction and implantation – on real patients. This is not a simulation. This is not a cadaver lab. This is live surgery under expert supervision.

Most training courses offered by organizations like ASAHRS or DHI Academy charge comparable or higher fees for observation-only programs. You watch a surgeon work through a screen or from across the room. At HTTC, you hold the instruments. You extract grafts. You implant them. You learn by doing – the only way to truly develop surgical skills.

Turkey is one of the few countries where hands-on training on real patients is legally permitted, which is why leading surgeons from the USA, Europe, the Middle East, Latin America and Asia choose HTTC for their training.

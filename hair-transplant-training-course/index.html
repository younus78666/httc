FUE Training Course

Hair transplant training course for doctors and surgeons

The HTTC Hair Transplant Training Center in Ankara, Turkey provides comprehensive, hands-on FUE training for licensed physicians, dermatologists and surgeons. Unlike most programs that limit participants to observation, HTTC participants perform actual extractions and implantations on real patients from day one, under the direct supervision of surgeons with over 20 years of FUE experience.

Who this training is for

The HTTC training course is designed for:

  • Physicians starting in hair restoration – build a solid foundation in FUE from the ground up with hands-on surgical practice
  • Experienced surgeons expanding their skills – refine advanced techniques including body hair transplant (BHT), repair cases and manual extraction methods
  • Dermatologists and plastic surgeons – add hair transplantation as a high-demand specialty to your practice
  • Clinic owners – train your surgical team in standardized, high-quality FUE methodology

What you will learn

The training program covers the complete hair transplant workflow, from initial patient assessment to post-operative care:

Theory

  • Hair and scalp anatomy, hair growth cycles, donor area physiology
  • Patient selection criteria and consultation techniques
  • Donor area management and long-term planning
  • Hairline design principles, natural angles, density distribution, age-appropriate design
  • Graft survival factors and storage solutions

Hands-on surgical training

  • Manual FUE extraction – punch selection (0.6 mm to 0.9 mm), depth control, angle adaptation for different hair types (straight, curly, wavy)
  • Powered extraction – training with the PCID (Powered Cole Isolation Device) from Cole Instruments, achieving transection rates below 2.58%
  • Stick-and-place implantation – immediate implantation after channel creation for maximum graft survival
  • Body hair transplant (BHT) – extraction from beard (sub-chin area), chest and other donor sites
  • Slit creation – blade selection, channel depth and angle for optimal density and natural growth direction

Instrument training

  • Manual punch handles and sharp vs. dull punch techniques
  • The PCID device, setup, calibration, hands-on practice
  • Cole Instruments product line, surrounded punches, graft processing board, counting device
  • Implantation tools, forceps technique, implanters, stick-and-place methodology

A typical training day

  • Morning (08:00 – 09:00)

    Theoretical session: anatomy review, technique discussion, case planning for the day's patient(s).

  • Morning surgery (09:00 – 13:00)

    First surgical session. Participants perform FUE extractions under direct supervision. Real-time feedback on punch angle, depth and speed. Graft counting and quality assessment.

  • Lunch break (13:00 – 14:00)

    Review of morning performance, discussion of cases.

  • Afternoon surgery (14:00 – 18:00)

    Implantation session. Participants create recipient channels and perform stick-and-place implantation. Focus on density, angle and natural distribution.

  • Debrief (18:00 – 18:30)

    Review of the day, technique assessment, preparation for next day.

Why HTTC: real practice, not just observation

The critical difference between HTTC and virtually every other hair transplant training program in the world is simple: you operate. At HTTC, participants perform actual surgical steps, extraction and implantation, on real patients. This is not a simulation. This is not a cadaver lab. This is live surgery under expert supervision.

Most training courses offered by organizations like ASAHRS or DHI Academy charge comparable or higher fees for observation-only programs. You watch a surgeon work through a screen or from across the room. At HTTC, you hold the instruments. You extract grafts. You implant them. You learn by doing, the only way to truly develop surgical skills.

Turkey is one of the few countries where hands-on training on real patients is legally permitted, which is why leading surgeons from the USA, Europe, the Middle East, Latin America and Asia choose HTTC for their training.

Training outcomes and certificate

Upon completion of the training program, participants receive a Certificate of Completion signed by the HTTC instructors. Graduates leave with:

  • Practical surgical experience on real patients
  • Confidence in manual FUE extraction and stick-and-place implantation
  • Familiarity with the latest instruments including the PCID
  • A network of international colleagues from the HTTC alumni community
  • Ongoing support, former participants can contact HTTC for case consultations

Pricing and registration

Training costs USD 5,000 per day, including all hands-on practice, theoretical instruction, instruments and certificate. Sessions are available as one-on-one or small group (2-4 participants). Duration ranges from a 1-day intensive to a 4-week comprehensive program.

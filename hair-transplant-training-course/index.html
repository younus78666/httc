The curriculum

Exactly what you'll walk through.

From the first consultation to the final graft, you'll cover the whole workflow, then practise it for real.

The theory you actually use

  • Hair and scalp anatomy, growth cycles, donor physiology
  • Patient selection and consultation that sets expectations
  • Donor management and long-term planning
  • Hairline design: natural angles, density, age-appropriate
  • Graft survival factors and storage

The surgery, in your hands

  • Manual FUE extraction, punch 0.6-0.9 mm, depth and angle control
  • Powered extraction with the PCID, transection below 2.58%
  • Stick-and-place for maximum graft survival
  • Body hair transplant from beard, chest and more
  • Slit creation: blade, depth and angle for natural density

The instruments, hands-on

  • Manual punch handles, sharp vs. dull techniques
  • The PCID: setup, calibration and live practice
  • Cole Instruments line: punches, processing board, counter
  • Implantation tools, forceps and implanters

A day with us

Here's what your day looks like.

You'll start with theory and case planning, spend the day in surgery, and finish by reviewing your own work.

  • 08:00 – 09:00 · Plan

    Anatomy review, technique discussion and case planning for today's patient.

  • 09:00 – 13:00 · Extract

    You perform FUE extractions under direct supervision, with live feedback on punch angle, depth and speed.

  • 13:00 – 14:00 · Reset

    Lunch, plus a quick look back at how the morning went.

  • 14:00 – 18:00 · Implant

    You create recipient channels and perform stick-and-place implantation, focused on density, angle and natural flow.

  • 18:00 – 18:30 · Review

    We go through your work together and set up tomorrow.

Do I really operate on real patients?

Yes. From day one you perform real extractions and implantations on real patients, under the direct supervision of our surgeons. It's not observation, a cadaver lab or a simulation.

Is it for beginners or experienced surgeons?

Both. We tailor each session to your level, whether you're performing your first extraction or refining advanced techniques like body hair transplant and repair work.

How long is the training?

Whatever fits you, from a one-day intensive to a four-week comprehensive program. Tell us your goals and we'll suggest a length.

What does it cost, and what's included?

USD 5,000 per day, all inclusive: hands-on practice, theory, instruments and your certificate. Sessions are one-on-one or a small group of 2-4.

Do I get a certificate?

Yes, a Certificate of Completion signed by your HTTC instructors. See what it covers.

Why train in Turkey?

Turkey is one of the few countries where hands-on training on real patients is legally permitted, which is exactly why surgeons fly in from the USA, Europe, the Middle East, Latin America and Asia to train here.
